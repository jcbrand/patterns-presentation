Patternslib and Mockup

Breaking up and getting back together again.

A presentation by JC Brand

What is Patternslib?

What is Patternslib?

  • A front-end development paradigm and toolkit
  • A library of so-called interaction patterns

Huh?

Patternslib lets you enhance the functionality of your website, without having to know or care about Javascript.

Ok, so what is Mockup?

Mockup is a rewrite/fork, done specifically for Plone.

Why was it forked?

Let's investigate...

  • Scene:Balcony Hall of the Musis Sacrum, in Arnhem, the Netherlands.
  • Time:Friday, October 12, 2012.
  • The designer Cornelis Colbach is demoing Patternslib.
  • Plone developer Rok Garbas sees it, is excited by the possibilities and wants patterns for Plone.
    • December 2012: Rok starts Mockup ("patterns for plone") and integrates it into plone.app.widgets
    Early 2013: 2013: PLIP to include Mockup into core Plone. https://dev.plone.org/ticket/13476
    2013: Rok writes blog post:
    Mockup OK time to explain it!
    in which he explains the choice of using Patternslib as basis for widgets in Plone.
    • Some time in 2013:
      Mockup: I got upset with how Javascript is (ab)used in Plone. Not 100% sure where this project is heading, but we need to change our additude towards Javascript. I hope others feel similar and would join the effort.
      https://garbas.si/blog/2013/contribution-in-january.html
    • Some time in 2013: Rok makes a hard fork / rewrite of Mockup. Breaking compatibiilty with Patternslib and forging own path.
    • Sometime in 2014... Rok steps down as Mockup maintainer and becomes less active in Plone community.
    • Sometime in 2014... Nathan van Gheem takes over as Mockup maintainer (helped by Franco and Johannes)
    • In the meantime, Patternslib continues development and sees deployment in various sites.
    • Sometime... The Plone Intranet consortium is created and Plone Intranet is started. Based on Patternslib
    • So now we had two patterns libraries being used in the Plone community, duplicating each others functionality and incompatible with one another.
